Friday Comes Out on Top for Lottery Players

Friday has been the luckiest day to play the lottery, according to more than 20 years of Powerball and Mega Millions results.

Action Network compared how often each scheduled draw day produced a jackpot winner. Friday Mega Millions ranked first, followed by Wednesday and Saturday Powerball.

Mega Millions produced a jackpot winner in 9.81% of Friday draws analyzed. That was the highest rate of any day and lottery combination in the study.

Key Findings

  • Friday ranks as the luckiest day overall, with a Mega Millions jackpot winner in 9.81% of Friday draws analyzed.
  • Wednesday is the luckiest day for Powerball players, with a jackpot winner in 9.40% of Wednesday draws analyzed.
  • Friday beats Tuesday for Mega Millions, with 122 jackpot-winning draws compared with 104.
  • Wednesday only narrowly beats Saturday for Powerball, 9.40% to 9.31%.
  • Monday Powerball has delivered the biggest average winning jackpot, at about $518 million, although Monday draws only began in 2021.
  • Each jackpot draw was counted once, even when the prize was split between multiple winning tickets.

Does Playing on Friday Actually Improve Your Odds?

No. A Friday ticket does not have better mathematical odds than a ticket bought for another draw day.

The ranking looks at what happened historically. It shows which scheduled draw days produced jackpot winners most often, not which day gives an individual ticket better odds.

What the Data Tells Us

“Friday stands out in more than 20 years of results. Mega Millions jackpots were won more often on Fridays than on any other day and lottery combination we analyzed.

Powerball is much closer. Wednesday only just beats Saturday, making those 2 days almost impossible to separate historically.

These results do not change the odds of the next draw. They show where the jackpots have landed in the past.”

Action Network Data Analyst

Methodology

What we measured. Action Network analyzed more than 20 years of Powerball and Mega Millions jackpot results. Each jackpot draw was counted once, even when multiple tickets shared the prize.

How we ranked the days. We compared the number of jackpot-winning draws on each day with the total number of scheduled draws held on that day during the period analyzed.

Time period. The data covers Mega Millions results from May 17, 2002 to March 17, 2026 and Powerball results from February 1, 2003 to May 2, 2026. Monday Powerball draws were counted from August 23, 2021, when Monday drawings began.

Prize values. We used the advertised jackpot amount to compare average and record prizes.
